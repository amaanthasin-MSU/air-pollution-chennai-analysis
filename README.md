# 🌫️ Air Pollution Analysis in Chennai (PM2.5 Trends & Environmental Factors)

## 📌 Problem

Air pollution is a major public health concern in urban areas like Chennai. When I visited Chennai back in December 2025, I immedietly felt the impact pollution has had on the city upon stepping out the airplane. During my trip there, I spent time learning more about the reasons for pollution and decided to take it a step further by integrating my analytical skills into better understanding the pollution crisis. Understanding how pollution levels change over time—and what factors influence them—is critical for environmental policy and health planning.

---

## 🎯 Objective

Analyze PM2.5 trends and related pollutants to identify patterns, seasonal behavior, and environmental factors that contribute to poor air quality.

---

## 📊 Dataset

* Government air quality monitoring data (Chennai, 2024)
* Pollutants: PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, O₃
* Weather variables: temperature, humidity, wind speed, rainfall

---

## ⚙️ Approach

### Data Preparation

* Cleaned and standardized time-series data using Python (pandas)
* Handled missing values and inconsistencies
* Structured dataset for analysis

### Analysis

* Time-series analysis of PM2.5 levels
* Rolling averages to identify trends
* Comparison of pollution levels under different weather conditions
* Seasonal and event-based analysis (e.g., high pollution days)

### Visualization

* Line charts for trend analysis
* Comparative plots (rain vs no rain, wind speed impact)
* Comparitive plots showing Festival vs Non-Festival days and the aftermath
* Highlighted periods of elevated pollution

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* PM2.5 levels showed clear fluctuations over time, with identifiable peak periods
* Weather conditions such as rainfall and wind speed influenced pollution levels
* Certain environmental conditions were consistently associated with higher pollution
* Sustained exposure levels often exceeded recommended WHO guidelines
